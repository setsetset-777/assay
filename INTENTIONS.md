# Assay - Intention notes

## Goal
Allow product managers to understand needs of their client and align on specifications.

## Origin

One of the keys to a successful collaboration with a client is to understand their needs and translate them into clear specifications.

On one side it allows for designers to have all the material and tools to deliver a cohesive and relevant product. On the other side it assure that both parties agree on what the product will achieve and protect them of future disagreements.

## Domain

This tool is intentionally generic. It is designed to serve any individual, team, or organization that builds something on behalf of a client and needs to formalize that client's expectations into a workable brief.

Example of application:

- Communication agencies with communication support
- Freelance developer with website
- Interior designer with redesign project

The tool shall adapt to the nature of the project and the domain of the execoutioner.

## Process

### 1. Gather client input

Collect raw input from client through domain and company tailored tools and processes. The goal is to surface needs, expectations, constraints, and success criteria without filtering or interpreting prematurely.

Supports can be:

- questionnaires
- interviews
- workshop
- reference material
- conversational assistant

### 2. Analyze input

Once input has been gathered, it is reviewed and synthesized to identify relevant information and highlight contraints, priorities, themes, ambiguities, etc.

This step can be done manually. It also has the potential for automation with a tool given enough context to digest the raw data.

### 3. Write specifications

Translate the analyzed input into a formal specification document. This should cover scope, objectives, functional requirements, constraints, and any relevant non-functional expectations (e.g. timelines, quality standards, accessibility).

### 4. Iterate via feedback loop with client

The goal is to gain validation of the client on the product specification.

Parties shall be able to comment/suggest/edit/discuss the document via a unique interface.

If a party is not satisfied with the document current state, it can ask for a review. 

### 5. Validate specifications

Both parties agree on a final version of the specifications and individually mark it as accepted.

It can be “digitally signed” and timestamped.

After validation, the design process can start.

Client can also ask for a review of the document, which will trigger a new feedback iteration process.
